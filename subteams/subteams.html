Subteams

There are 7 main subteams that make up Penn Robotics. Each has their own distinct purpose, and without them, Penn wouldn't be able to do the things they do.

On our business subteam, work is primarily concerned with collecting funding for our team. This funding allows us to organize a budget and forecast the amount that should be spent on our robot, team, and community. Annually, around 80,000 dollars is raised on our team to put towards manufacturing, wiring, programming and designing a robot to compete with; a large portion of the funding also goes towards community outreach and helping people in need. Acquiring the funding forecasted usually stems from sponsorships, fundraisers, and grants. We send letters to local businesses, set up fundraisers like chicken sales, and apply for government grants to inch us closer to our set goal. At the end of each season, we take a chunk of what’s left, and pack it away for a boost in the coming season as well. Our business subteam is full of opportunity and allows students to learn the basics of business that can be applied in the real world.

Media’s job is to be the face of our team. In the media subteam, we use a variety of skills to present our team to the public in the best possible light. These skills include things like taking quality pictures, using photoshop, editing videos, and making posts on social media.

Media is responsible for all of the visual content our team puts out, so it is crucial that we have a large repository of pictures at our disposal at all times. At least one representative from the media team should be present at all outreach events so that documentation can take place. At an outreach event, some good things to take pictures of include:

  • Members of our team at the event
  • Members of our team interacting with the community
  • Members of our team interacting with other FIRST teams
  • Noteworthy landmarks or signs in the area for context in social media posts
  • People from the community interacting with a robot
  • Robots interacting with people from the community

    • Photography is also fantastic during meeting times. Some things that would be good to have documented include:

  • Pictures of team members working at desks
  • Pictures of team members mentoring FTC teams
  • Pictures of people working in the machine shop (Be careful, wear safety glasses, and let them know what you are doing. Distracting someone on heavy machinery can be deadly.)

    • On our software team, work is split between both programming and wiring. Linked within the WPILib website is the API documentation for your desired programming language (Java or C++). Some wiring information is also on the previously mentioned WPILib website.
